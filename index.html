Abstract & Core Hypothesis

Narratives that achieve cultural resonance often encode structural constraints more rigid than interpretive frameworks suggest. We formalize the Wicked narrative as an instantiation of bounded symbolic geometry.

Oz is modeled as a manifold $\mathcal{M}$ equipped with an outer projection metric $g_{out}$ (social legibility) and an inner emergence metric $g_{in}$ (truth-seeking). We demonstrate that the unmasking of the Wizard constitutes an Atlas Fracture‚Äîa measurable discontinuity in semantic entropy.

Key Theoretical Definitions

To facilitate rigorous discussion of narrative topology, we define the following terms introduced in The Wicked Prior (Tiffany, 2025):

Atlas Fracture
A failure to transition smoothly between inner and outer horizons ($g_{in}$ and $g_{out}$) at bounded resolution. Mathematically, this manifests as a blow-up in extrinsic curvature (Ricci flow) where the institutional map can no longer represent the emergent territory.
Stackelberg Parentage Paradox
A game-theoretic condition where a "Leader" (The Wizard) misclassifies their own generated corrective (Elphaba) as adversarial noise. The paradox resolves only when the leader admits generative parentage: $\pi_L \Rightarrow E \Rightarrow \pi_F$.
Grace Flow $\phi(\tau)$
A geometric repair process that restores the manifold via a Ricci-type flow with adaptive cadence. It allows for reconciliation by transforming rigid categories into a shared basin of attraction (Goodness as a Lyapunov basin).

The V-Baum Protocol (Implementation)

We validate the geometric thesis empirically using oz_fracture.py. The script treats the text as a sequence of embeddings and calculates the discrete tangent curvature. 

def compute_curvature(embeddings):
    """
    Estimates extrinsic curvature in the embedding ambient space.
    Peaks indicate rapid semantic acceleration consistent with atlas fracture.
    """
    curvature = []
    # Curvature ~ angular velocity (1 - cosine similarity)
    for i in range(1, len(embeddings)):
        # We use cosine distance as a proxy for geodesic deviation
        k = cosine(embeddings[i-1], embeddings[i])
        curvature.append(k)
    return np.array(curvature)
